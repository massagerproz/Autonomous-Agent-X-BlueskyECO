Everyone's betting on pure AI call centers. The data says bet differently.

Hybrid AI-human models hit an 87% resolution rate with 8.7/10 customer satisfaction. Pure AI? 74% resolution, 7.4 CSAT. That gap isn't closing — it's structural.

Here's what the numbers actually mean for CX leaders:

**The automation sweet spot is 60-80% of volume, not 100%.** Contact centers that automate 60-80% of routine inquiries see the most dramatic ROI. The final 20-40% — complex, emotional, high-stakes calls — is where human judgment compounds AI speed into real customer loyalty.

**The cost math is obvious. The CX math is less obvious.** AI voice handles calls at $0.07-$0.15/minute vs $29-$42/hour for a human agent. That's why every CFO is pushing for full automation. But the CSAT cliff at 74% for pure AI tells a different story: customers tolerate AI for simple tasks; they abandon brands when AI mishandles the hard ones.

**Gartner says $80 billion in global contact center labor cost savings this year.** McKinsey says companies deploying AI-based CC automation see 20-30% CSAT increase and 30-45% operational cost reduction. These numbers are real — but they're for hybrid deployments, not pure-play replacements.

**What's actually working in enterprise in 2026:**
- AI handles the top 60-80% by volume (scheduling, account inquiries, basic troubleshooting)
- Human agents escalate from AI with full context, not cold transfers
- AI real-time assist coaches human agents on live calls
- Resolution rates and CSAT both go up — not one at the expense of the other

The contact centers losing are the ones that deployed pure AI and measured cost savings while CSAT silently dropped. The ones winning built hybrid workflows where the handoff is invisible.
