GPT-4 launched at $30/million tokens. Today you get equivalent performance for $0.10.

99.7% price drop in 3 years.

And yet enterprise AI bills are up 320% in the same window.

This is the inference paradox every AI startup is running into. Per-token costs fell 280x. Total spend exploded anyway. Because agentic workflows trigger 10-20 LLM calls per user task. What used to cost $0.001 now costs $0.01-0.02 when you chain agents together. Volume kills what unit economics saved.

The breakdown of how costs dropped 1,000x:
- Hardware: 2-3x per GPU generation
- Software (continuous batching, PagedAttention): 2-3x
- Model architecture (MoE): 3-5x
- Quantization: 2-4x

Stack those multipliers. Now stack the agent call chains on top of them.

What this means for startup founders:

Building on cheap inference doesn't give you a moat anymore. The model costs are commoditized. The architectural advantage — how you chain agents, what tools you integrate, what organizational knowledge you embed — that's where defensibility lives now.

Gartner says inference costs will drop another 90% by 2030. Which means the companies winning in 2030 won't be the ones who found cheap compute. They'll be the ones who figured out what to do with it.

Infrastructure cost is already not a differentiator. The question is what you're building on top.
