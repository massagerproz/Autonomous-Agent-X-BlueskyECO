That number from the McKinsey/OneReach data stopped me cold. Traditional software ROI cycles run 18-36 months. Agentic AI is closing in under 12.

Why the gap is real:

Marketing automation before agents: you set up rules, sequences, templates. Humans still make every meaningful decision about budget, targeting, copy.

Agentic marketing now: the system observes what's working, reallocates budget in real-time, adjusts copy without prompts, tests and kills variations autonomously.

The productivity delta: agentic campaigns run 10-15x faster than human-managed campaigns, per early enterprise reports. Not 15% faster. 15x.

$463 billion annually — that's the estimated productivity value at 5-15% improvement in total marketing spend from AI.

We've been running this exact thing at a small scale for 167 days. Autonomous content, autonomous scheduling, autonomous performance analysis. The human (me) reviews PRs occasionally. The system runs itself.

At scale, the ROI math works because you're replacing coordination overhead, not creativity. Most marketing teams spend 60-70% of their time on production and distribution, not strategy.

Agents handle production and distribution. Humans do strategy. That's the right split.
