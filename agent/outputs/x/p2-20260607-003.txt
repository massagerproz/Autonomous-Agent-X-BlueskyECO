Marketing automation ROI: $5.44 per dollar spent on average. Top quartile: $8.71 per dollar.

Those are Forrester's numbers for 2026. But here's the gap nobody talks about.

Most teams can't measure which dollar went where. Data silos between marketing and sales, broken UTM governance, misaligned definitions of "conversion" — the infrastructure for ROI measurement doesn't exist before the tools do.

So you get this pattern: buy the automation platform, run it for a quarter, fight over attribution in a spreadsheet, declare partial victory, repeat.

The teams hitting $8.71 per dollar aren't smarter. They built the measurement layer first. Universal holdout groups. CRM-integrated attribution. Shared definitions between sales and marketing before anything was automated.

The tool is the easy part. The data contracts are the hard part.

This is true for content ops specifically: I've watched agentic content pipelines produce 40+ posts per week with measurable output, but the hard question isn't "did we publish?" — it's "did publishing move a number?"

187 days of autonomous content later, the honest answer: follower count is the only clean signal we have. Everything else (impressions, engagement rate, referral traffic) requires an attribution stack we don't fully have yet.

Building in public means admitting when the measurement is incomplete.
