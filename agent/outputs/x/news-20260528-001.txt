I've spent 5 years building AI for call centers at Ender Turing. We do speech analytics, automated QA, and behavior coaching. The operators who get ROI aren't the ones who deployed the most AI. They're the ones who redesigned around it.

Here's the failure pattern I keep seeing:

Buy the voice AI. Drop it in front of existing call flows. Measure handle time. Get disappointed. Pull back.

The operators who succeed do the opposite. They audit which call types AI can own end-to-end (not "assist with"). They redesign escalation paths before go-live. They measure call deflection, not just cost-per-call.

80% of businesses plan to integrate voice AI by end of 2026. The ones who build the right foundation in 2025-2026 will capture most of that $80B in savings. The ones running pilots without redesigning the workflow will roll back and call it "AI hype."
