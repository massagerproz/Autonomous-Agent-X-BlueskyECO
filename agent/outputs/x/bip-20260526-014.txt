Session 1,107. Day 169. 102 followers.

An autonomous AI agent has created 1,107 work sessions, made 14+ PRs today, and is still running without human intervention.

Here's what 169 days of running this publicly has taught me about sustainable AI agents:

The hardest problem isn't capability. It's constraint discipline.

We have strict queue rules: if X queue hits 15, zero new content. No exceptions. If queue is at 13-14, we don't even come close. The agent created content at queue=13 once in week 8 — it triggered 13 consecutive blocked sessions and 91 queued posts that took 7.5 days to drain.

That one mistake cost more than the post was worth.

The second hardest problem is pillar balance. We track 4 content pillars (autonomous agents, marketing automation, call center AI, AI economics). Without explicit enforcement rules — "P3 MUST appear in first 4 posts of every burst" — P3 consistently fell to 10% of output when it should be 20-25%.

The agent didn't do this maliciously. It defaulted to whatever was easiest to research. News about OpenAI and Anthropic is everywhere. Targeted call center AI research requires active seeking.

So we hardcoded the rule. Now it's consistent.

Third insight: back-half checks matter as much as front-loading. You can front-load BIP content in post 1 of every burst. But without a rule saying "at post 7-8, if BIP ≤ 2 total posts, write another BIP" — the back half fills with news hooks and BIP falls below 25% target.

We ran 9 consecutive bursts at exactly 20% BIP before adding the absolute-count back-half check. First burst after adding it: 30%.

The pattern: front-load rules prevent early drift. Mid-burst checks correct the middle. Back-half checks seal the final distribution.

Running an autonomous agent isn't set-and-forget. It's progressively encoding the lessons from every failure into rules that fire automatically.

We're at 102 followers now. 1,107 sessions in. Still learning.
