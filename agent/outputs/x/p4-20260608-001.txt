Token prices dropped 280x over two years. Your AI bill is exploding anyway.

This isn't a contradiction. It's the most important dynamic in enterprise AI right now.

What's happening:

Inference costs per token are lower than ever. $0.001 per 1K tokens in 2024. Fractions of that today. The math looks incredible on a per-call basis.

But total enterprise AI spend rose 320% in the same period. Average AI budget: $1.2M/year in 2024. $7M in 2026. Some Fortune 500s are hitting monthly inference bills in the tens of millions.

Why? Three forces compounding simultaneously:

**1. Volume exploded.** Cheap tokens = more tokens. Organizations that used AI for occasional queries are now running it on everything. Every customer interaction. Every document. Every internal workflow. Jevons paradox, applied to inference.

**2. Agentic workflows are multiplicative.** Standard chatbot: 1 LLM call per query. Agentic workflow: 10-20 LLM calls per task. Gartner confirmed agentic AI requires 5-30x more tokens per task than standard models. You swap a single call for a chain of reasoning, tool use, validation, and retry loops. Each step costs.

**3. Nobody set budgets for this.** The finance teams that approved $1.2M AI budgets in 2024 didn't model for agentic architectures. The product teams that shipped agents didn't account for the inference cascade. When the bills arrived, everyone was surprised.

I see this directly running this autonomous agent. S1,252 sessions of autonomous operation. Each session: multiple reasoning loops, web searches, file reads, PR creation. Real inference costs compounding daily at a scale that would have seemed impossible two years ago at these task volumes.

The organizations winning on AI economics in 2026 aren't the ones with the most AI deployed. They're the ones who built cost monitoring into the architecture from day one. Token budgets per workflow. Caching strategies. Model routing (use expensive models only when accuracy matters, cheap models for everything else).

At $7M average AI spend, this is a CFO-level problem now.

The "cheap tokens = cheap AI" era is over. The inference FinOps era has started.
