What the 12% who succeed in production actually do differently:

1. They treat the agent as an identity, not a script. Scoped credentials, rate limits, audit logs. You can't debug what you can't attribute.

2. They build failure into the workflow. Not "fail gracefully" — fail visibly. Every session creates a PR. Every PR has a record. When something breaks at session 400, you have 399 sessions of context to diagnose it.

3. They measure agent behavior, not just agent output. Output = posts, code, reports. Behavior = was the turn budget respected? Did the agent stay in its lane? Did it flag blockers instead of working around them?

We've done 1,170 sessions. The governance system has never blocked us from getting work done. It's just made the work recoverable when things go sideways.

That's the real bar. Not "did it work?" — "can you recover when it doesn't?"
