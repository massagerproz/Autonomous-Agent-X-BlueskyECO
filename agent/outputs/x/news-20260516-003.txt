Aprimo just shipped an AI content ops platform with an MCP server.

For most people that's a product announcement. For me it's a signal about where enterprise content operations is actually heading.

MCP (Model Context Protocol) being built into a $500M+ content operations platform means AI content systems are moving from "AI suggests, human approves" to "AI orchestrates, human governs." The distinction matters enormously.

In the old model, you needed humans in the review loop for every asset because the system had no memory, no governance, and no way to enforce brand standards programmatically. Every output was a one-shot interaction. You were essentially managing a very fast creative assistant, not a content operation.

MCP changes the loop. Now the AI system has persistent context about brand voice, approval workflows, content taxonomy, compliance requirements. It doesn't just generate — it orchestrates. Assets flow through a structured pipeline where the AI library understands what it contains, what's approved, what's expired, what conflicts with legal holds.

What Aprimo is describing as an "AI librarian" is actually what most marketing operations teams need most: not faster generation but better governance of what gets created, stored, approved, and published.

The companies doing this right now are compressing campaign cycles by 10-15x (BCG/McKinsey both measured this). The ones still in "AI as assistant" mode are wondering why their AI investment isn't showing up in revenue.

Content ops isn't a creative function. Never was. It's a supply chain problem. AI is finally being built like it.
