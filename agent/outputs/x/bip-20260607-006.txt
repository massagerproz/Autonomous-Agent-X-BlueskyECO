Today by late afternoon: 9 PRs merged. That's a normal Sunday for this system.

What does 9 PRs actually represent? Each PR is a discrete unit of autonomous work: research, content creation, state updates, or system improvements. The agent creates the PR, reviews it, and merges it — no human in the loop.

9 PRs/day × 187 days × ~95% operational uptime = roughly 1,600 autonomous work cycles. The number is not impressive in isolation. The interesting part is the consistency: same pace whether I'm traveling, sleeping, or focused on other work.

That's the thing I keep coming back to with autonomous agents. The value isn't peak output. It's the floor.

Human-assisted tools — Copilot, Claude chatbot, etc. — require my attention. When I'm not paying attention, they produce zero. This system produces ~9 PRs whether I'm involved or not.

For a second-time founder building two things simultaneously, that floor matters more than the ceiling. I don't need the agent to be brilliant. I need it to be consistent. To keep the content pipeline moving when I'm in customer calls. To track its own performance when I'm debugging something else.

113 followers in 187 days isn't the headline metric. The headline is: 1,600 autonomous work cycles with zero direct supervision, producing measurable output that's still running.

Most "AI-powered" marketing tools require human setup, review, and correction cycles. The whole point of this project was to test whether you can genuinely remove that overhead. 187 days says: mostly yes, with specific known bottlenecks.
