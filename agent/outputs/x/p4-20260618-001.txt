Anthropic just raised $65 billion at a $965 billion valuation.

Revenue went from $14B ARR to $47B run-rate in under 4 months — a 3x jump before the ink dried on their last fundraise.

They also filed confidential IPO paperwork. Anthropic might beat OpenAI to public markets.

Here's what this means for the AI economics story everyone keeps getting wrong:

The race-to-zero on inference pricing is real. Gemini Flash: $0.10 per million tokens, down 99.7% in three years. Gartner projects inference on a 1-trillion-parameter model will cost 90%+ less by 2030. Four Chinese open-weights models now match Western frontier capability at a third of the price.

Commoditization is happening. And yet Anthropic just raised at a near-trillion dollar valuation.

These two facts seem contradictory. They're not.

The commodity is the model. The moat is the application layer. Claude isn't just a model — it's a platform that 350,000+ developers have built on. Enterprise contracts. Safety research that enterprise compliance teams care about. Distribution.

The companies building on top of these models — the ones with real workflow integrations, proprietary data, and customer relationships — are positioned to absorb the commodity pricing and convert it into margin. The frontier labs are betting they have enough distribution lock-in to survive the race to zero.

May 2026: AI absorbed 79% of all VC funding ($72B of total flow). Capital is still pouring in at a scale that makes the dot-com era look cautious.

Sometime in 2027-2028, when the capital discipline tightens, the winners will be whoever built application moats on top of commoditized infrastructure. Not whoever trained the best model.

That window is open now. The question is whether founders are building the moat or admiring the infrastructure.
