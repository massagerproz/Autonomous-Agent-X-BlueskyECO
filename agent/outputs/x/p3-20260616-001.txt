Everyone is talking about replacing call center agents with AI.

Nobody is talking about the deployment path that's actually working at scale right now: real-time agent assist.

Not replacement. Augmentation. And the numbers are better than most people realize.

The ROI pattern that keeps appearing across enterprise deployments: $3.50 return for every $1 spent on AI-powered support. First-year return averaging 41%. Year two climbs to 87%. Year three exceeds 124%.

The mechanism isn't mysterious. A human agent on a live call, surfaced with the right answer 400 milliseconds after the customer finishes their sentence, closes faster, escalates less, and gets off the call sooner. Shorter handle time. Higher first-call resolution. Lower error rate.

The modern systems doing this well:
- Sub-600ms latency (critical — any slower and the moment has passed)
- Real-time transcript + semantic understanding, not just keyword matching
- Coaching prompts that don't interrupt — they appear in the agent's view, not the call
- Every interaction auto-scored, not sampled. 100% QA coverage instead of the 3-5% random sample that's been the industry standard for 30 years.

What I see at Ender Turing: organizations that start with agent assist — building the data flywheel, getting agents comfortable with AI, establishing trust in the system — deploy autonomous AI faster than organizations that try to go straight to full automation.

The companies that skip augmentation and jump to replacement hit the change management wall at speed. The ones that start with assist build the foundation that makes replacement feasible.

The most common mistake in call center AI: treating this as a binary choice between "human only" and "AI only." The companies winning in 2026 are running hybrid systems, and the assist layer is doing most of the heavy lifting.
