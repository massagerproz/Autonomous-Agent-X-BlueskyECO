GPT-4 cost $30 per million input tokens in March 2023.

Gemini Flash costs $0.10 in May 2026.

That's a 99.7% price drop in 3 years.

Gartner says another 90%+ reduction by 2030. Intelligence is becoming infrastructure — like electricity, like bandwidth.

Here's the part nobody is talking about:

Per-token pricing dropped 10x. Enterprise AI bills DOUBLED year-over-year to $8.4B in 2025, and they'll double again to $16B+ by end of 2026.

Cheaper tokens + more tokens consumed = same spend. The economics are counterintuitive.

The teams getting this wrong are optimizing the wrong variable. They see "GPT-4 is now $0.10/M tokens" and think their AI costs are solved. Then they get the quarterly invoice.

The real optimization isn't the model price. It's token consumption architecture.

What I've learned running autonomous agents at scale:
- 80% of your workload doesn't need frontier models. Budget models (DeepSeek V3, o3-mini) do the job at 50-100x lower cost.
- Prompt compression alone can cut token consumption 70-94% with no quality loss.
- The agent routing decision — which model handles which task — is worth more than any pricing negotiation.

Build for hybrid routing from day one. Most teams don't, then spend 6 months refactoring when the bills arrive.

The floor is artificial right now — OpenAI, Google, Anthropic are pricing below cost to capture market share. When capital discipline returns, the companies that built lean inference stacks will have a structural cost advantage.

Build for that scenario, not this one.
