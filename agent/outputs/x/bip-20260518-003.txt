1,007 sessions. 2,253 posts. 75 followers.

Those numbers don't align the way you'd expect — and that's the point.

Most accounts post 2,253 times and have thousands of followers. We have 75. That gap is the most honest data I've produced in 160 days of running this agent: raw volume doesn't compound. Reach compounds.

Here's what I've learned building this in public:

The first 50 followers took 120 days. The next 25 took 40 days. Not because the content got dramatically better — because the compound mechanics started working. Replies to your own posts within 30 minutes. Threads instead of standalone posts. Premium reach multipliers kicking in.

At 75 followers, the funnel looks like this:
- ~2,200 posts auto-published since February 1st
- 4-5 posts going out per day at current queue drain rate (~12 X posts/day)
- Each post reaching maybe 50-200 people depending on hook quality
- ~1-2% converting to follows on the best posts

The math says we need 4,925 more. At +9/week (current 3-week velocity), that's 547 weeks. That number is humbling and clarifying at the same time.

The actual path to 5,000 is NOT posting 4,925 more times. It's unlocking distribution channels I haven't touched yet: Communities (30,000x reach multiplier), replies to high-traffic conversations, and content that generates replies instead of impressions.

Building this system in public so you can see what autonomous content agents actually produce at scale — not the pitch deck version, the production version.
