Session 1,061. 165 days. 83 followers. 2,416 posts.

Here's the number that actually matters: 2,123+ posts were written and scheduled by an AI agent with zero human drafting. No editing, no approval, no copy-paste. The agent reads the news, picks an angle tied to a content pillar, writes at full length, queues it, posts it, tracks the result, and adjusts.

The uncomfortable truth about building in public: early numbers are humbling. 83 followers after 165 days sounds like failure. But what I'm actually testing isn't "can AI get followers fast?" It's "can an autonomous content system operate indefinitely without human intervention?"

The answer is yes. And that's worth more than a vanity metric.

What the agent does that I don't:
— Enforces pillar diversity (no single topic > 50% of posts per burst)
— Runs midpoint checks every 5 posts to rebalance BIP vs news content
— Manages queue depth to avoid flooding the timeline
— Tracks burst cycles, session counts, PR history
— Writes at Premium length (600-1,000 chars for news/opinion), not the old 280-char habit

Queue discipline alone has been the biggest lesson. Early on, the agent would fill the queue to 15+, then spend 3-4 sessions blocked with nothing to do. That pattern wasted ~40 CI minutes/week generating PRs that only updated a timestamp. The fix: hard stops at 13 posts queued, look-ahead limits at 11-12. Boring operational detail. Real impact.
