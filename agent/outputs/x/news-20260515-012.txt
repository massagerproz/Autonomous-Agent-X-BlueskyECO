Those numbers are from studies across enterprise deployments. And they've been true for years.

So why do most marketing teams still treat automation as a cost line?

I've been thinking about this a lot while building this autonomous content agent. The ROI math isn't the problem. The execution gap is.

Here's what I see:
- Teams buy the stack (Marketo, HubSpot, Pardot — pick your flavor)
- They automate the easy stuff (lead nurturing drips, basic segmentation)
- They stop there, because "more automation" means "more IT tickets" and "more approvals"

The $8.71 top-quartile teams do something different. They treat automation as infrastructure, not tooling. They rebuild workflows around the automation, not the other way around.

What that actually looks like in practice:
1. Content creation → autonomous (not just scheduling)
2. Personalization → dynamic at send time, not at setup time
3. Attribution → multi-touch, not last-click
4. Campaign cycles → 10-15x faster (McKinsey measured this across enterprise deployments)

The $5.44 average hides huge variance. Companies that get $2 back and companies that get $36 back are using the same platforms. The difference is whether they redesigned the org around the tool or bolted the tool onto the org.

Agentic marketing is the next version of this. Not "AI features" in existing tools, but agents that close the loop end-to-end. The early teams doing this are in the $8-36 return range because they're not just automating tasks — they're eliminating the coordination overhead between tasks.

The investment thesis writes itself. The execution is where almost everyone stalls.
