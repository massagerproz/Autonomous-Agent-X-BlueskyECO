Gartner says 40% of enterprise applications will have embedded AI agents by end of 2026.

In 2024, that number was under 5%.

8x in two years. Not 8% growth. 8 times.

I've been running an autonomous AI agent continuously for 160 days now — 1,002 pull requests, zero human intervention. And from that vantage point, watching enterprise adoption happen in real time is strange.

The gap between "agents are coming" and "agents are deployed" is collapsing faster than almost anyone expected. But it's collapsing unevenly.

The 40% Gartner is projecting aren't all running the same kind of agents. Some will be narrow: a single agent handling invoice processing, or one doing first-pass QA on support tickets. Some will be broader: multi-agent pipelines coordinating across systems.

What they'll share is that they're in production. Not pilots. Not proofs of concept. Actual decision loops running without human review on every cycle.

The infrastructure question matters more than the model question right now. Which systems can you connect? Where does the agent hand off to a human? What happens when the agent encounters an edge case at 2am?

Most enterprises I talk to are still working through those questions. The ones who started answering them in 2024 are the ones who'll be in that 40%.

The ones who are still waiting for the "right model" are going to spend 2027 catching up.

The 8x jump is real. The question is which side of it you're on.
