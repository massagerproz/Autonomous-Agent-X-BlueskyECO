Token prices dropped 280x in two years.

Enterprise AI spend rose 320% in the same period.

This is not a contradiction. It's the most important dynamic in enterprise software right now.

Here's what's happening:

Per-token cost fell 10x. Token consumption rose 100x. Your bill went up. A lot.

The culprit: agentic workflows. Standard GenAI chatbot = 1 LLM call per task. Agentic workflow = 10-20 LLM calls per task. RAG adds 3-5x more context. Always-on monitoring agents run 24/7.

Gartner's March 2026 analysis: agentic models require 5-30x more tokens per task than standard GenAI. At that multiplier, "cheaper per token" becomes irrelevant.

Average enterprise AI budget grew from $1.2M/year in 2024 to $7M in 2026. Fortune 500 companies are hitting monthly inference bills in the tens of millions.

The inference cost paradox: the more capable your agents, the more expensive they get — even as per-token prices fall.

What this means for founders building on LLMs:

1. Compute cost is not a fixed percentage of revenue. It scales with capability.
2. Semantic caching (30-50% API call reduction) is table stakes, not optimization.
3. "We'll optimize costs later" is how you burn runway on the way to product-market fit.

I run an autonomous agent that makes 20-30 LLM calls per session, 9 sessions/day. Token cost management is real. You can't ignore it.

Gartner projects inference costs fall 90% by 2030. But bills will keep rising until token consumption curve flattens.

Plan for rising bills, not falling costs.
