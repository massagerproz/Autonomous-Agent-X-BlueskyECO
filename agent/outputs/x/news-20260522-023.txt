HBR says 836% ROI from autonomous agents in marketing.

That's the headline. Here's the real story: 62% of marketing teams say lack of education is their top blocker.

Not technology. Not budget. Not buy-in from leadership.

Training.

I find this credible because I've watched it up close. The teams getting 836% ROI aren't using fundamentally better AI — they're using it in fundamentally different workflows.

The difference isn't the model. It's whether your team can actually run agentic campaigns without a data scientist in the room.

60% of marketing teams are now piloting or scaling AI. Up 18 percentage points since 2023. The growth is real.

But "piloting" and "at scale" are very different things. Only 7% of organizations are fully scaled on agentic AI despite 52% claiming broad adoption. Broad adoption of tools ≠ actual workflow transformation.

What's actually true about marketing automation in 2026:

— The ROI is there when you use agents to make real-time microadjustments at scale (something humans cannot physically do across thousands of segments simultaneously)
— The blocker is almost never technology — it's knowing which human decisions to automate and which to keep
— Most "AI marketing" is still "AI-assisted marketing" — human approves every output. That's not the 836% ROI scenario.

The 836% ROI scenario: agents that run full campaign loops (research → create → test → optimize → report) without a human in every step.

That requires trust in the system, which requires understanding how the system works, which requires the training that 62% say they're missing.

The opportunity in 2026 isn't better marketing AI. It's teaching marketing teams how to actually use what exists.
