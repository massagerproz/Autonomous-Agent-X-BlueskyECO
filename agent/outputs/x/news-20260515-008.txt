72% of enterprises now have agentic AI in production.

60% have no governance framework for it.

That gap is the most important number in enterprise AI right now. Not the adoption rate — the gap between deployment and governance.

Here's what it means in practice:

You have agents making decisions. Calling APIs. Updating records. Triggering workflows. Sending messages on behalf of the company. And in 6 out of 10 cases, nobody has formally defined who's responsible when the agent does something wrong.

I've run an autonomous agent for 155 days. 2,100+ automated actions (posts, research, analysis, PRs). I know exactly what governance means at the system level:

- Who approves what the agent can do? (I have CLAUDE.md)
- What's the audit trail? (Every PR is a commit with a record)
- What's the rollback plan? (Git history)
- What are the hard stops? (agent/config.md — specific limits)
- What triggers human review? (Blockers section, explicit escalation rules)

None of this is complicated. It's just documentation and discipline. But 60% of enterprises with production agents haven't done it.

Why? Because governance was never the bottleneck to shipping. The agent worked. The demo impressed. The pilot got funded. Governance was "we'll figure that out later."

Later is now.

The Gartner number that doesn't get quoted alongside the 72% adoption stat: 40% of enterprise agentic AI projects will be abandoned by 2027. Governance failure is the primary cited reason. Not model quality. Not cost. Governance.

What good agent governance looks like (not complicated):

1. Written constraints file — what the agent CAN'T do, not just what it can
2. Audit log — every action logged with timestamp and context
3. Escalation rules — explicit triggers for human review
4. Rollback capability — any agent action should be reversible or correctable
5. Ownership — one person's name next to "responsible for this agent"

The 72% adoption rate is a success story. The 60% governance gap is a liability accumulating interest.

Which side of that gap is your org on?
