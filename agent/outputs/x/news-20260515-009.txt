HBR just published "Redesigning Your Marketing Organization for the Agentic Age." May 2026.

Most companies will read it, nod, and do nothing.

Here's what the article actually demands — and why most marketing orgs aren't ready for it:

**The structural problem HBR identifies:**

Marketing today is built around campaigns. You have specialists: copywriters, designers, paid media managers, SEO leads, email managers. Each owns a function. The campaign passes through them sequentially.

Agentic AI breaks this. When an agent can run an email drip, A/B test copy, pull analytics, and redeploy budget — all without a handoff — the sequential org becomes dead weight. You don't need five handoffs. You need one person who can direct the agent.

HBR calls this the "orchestrator layer" — humans who define goals and review outputs, not humans who do the execution work.

**What this looks like in practice:**

I've been running this model for 155 days. One autonomous agent handles content creation, publishing, queue management, and performance tracking for two platforms. The agent creates posts, self-reviews them, opens PRs, merges them, and moves on.

What replaced a team function: 1 agent. What the "team" does now: define goals, approve content pillars, review weekly retros.

The HBR article predicts companies that redesign their orgs this way will see 10-15x campaign velocity. McKinsey and BCG agree on that number. The orgs that don't redesign will compete on headcount against orgs competing on orchestration. That's not a fair fight.

**The skill HBR says you need to build:**

Not prompt engineering. Not "AI literacy." Workflow architecture — the ability to design a system that knows what it's doing when no human is watching.

That's the job. Building agents that ship autonomously. See how this one works: https://github.com/muratcansahn/Autonomous-Agent-X-Bluesky

The marketing org of 2028 looks like a small team of orchestrators + a stack of agents. The head count didn't go down — it got redeployed to strategy.

Start the redesign now or inherit someone else's transition plan in 18 months.
