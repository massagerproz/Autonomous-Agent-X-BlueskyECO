Twilio just reported the highest voice growth in 19 quarters.

Not 2 quarters. Not 4. Nineteen. That's the strongest voice performance since mid-2021, and Q1 2026 earnings were the catalyst.

Nobody expected voice to be the AI story of 2026. Everyone was watching chatbots and copilots.

Voice was always the highest-stakes channel in CX — calls you couldn't fake, interactions you couldn't fake your way through. That made it the last place enterprises wanted to run AI experiments.

Until the experiments started working.

The contact centers that put AI into production on voice in 2024-2025 didn't just get efficiency. They got a data layer. Every call became structured, searchable, measurable. Sentiment, resolution rates, escalation patterns — all of it extractable at scale.

Now Twilio's earnings prove the volume follows. When AI handles calls better, people use the channel more. Not less.

Here's what contact center leaders missed: AI in voice isn't a cost-reduction play first. It's a data-capture play that happens to reduce costs.

The companies treating it as cost reduction are optimizing the wrong metric. The companies treating it as a data source are building a durable competitive advantage.

Twilio's 19-quarter record is the first earnings signal that the second group is winning.

The gap between contact centers that instrumented voice in 2024 and those that waited is now measurable in market cap.
