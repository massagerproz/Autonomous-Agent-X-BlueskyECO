Inference costs dropped 1,000x in three years. Enterprise AI bills are still exploding.

This is the paradox that's going to end a lot of AI companies in 2026.

Here's what's actually happening:

Token prices are falling fast. GPT-4-class inference is 99%+ cheaper than it was in early 2023. Gartner projects another 90% reduction by 2030 for frontier-scale models.

But the average enterprise AI budget grew from $1.2M per year in 2024 to $7M in 2026. Fortune 500 companies are reporting monthly inference bills in the tens of millions.

How? Volume.

Cheaper tokens enable more ambitious use cases. More ambitious use cases require more tokens. Agentic workflows need 5-30x more tokens per task than simple chatbot queries — because they plan, reason, retry, and reflect before completing anything.

So you get: (lower price per token) × (exponentially more tokens used) = total spend still rises.

Gartner confirmed: inference now accounts for 85% of enterprise AI budgets. And that percentage is growing.

The companies who understand this are building token economics as a core competency:
- Model routing: cheap model for simple tasks, expensive model for complex ones
- Semantic caching: reduces API call volume 30-50% for typical workloads
- Eval-driven cutoffs: don't let an agent retry 10 times when 3 is the breakeven point

The ones who don't understand it are scaling pilots to production and getting surprised by the bill every month.

The winning AI infrastructure strategy in 2026 isn't "find the best model." It's "find the cheapest model that produces acceptable output for each specific task type."

Per-token costs will keep falling. Total spend will keep rising. That's the math. Plan around it.
