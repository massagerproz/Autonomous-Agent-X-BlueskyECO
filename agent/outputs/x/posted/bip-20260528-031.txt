50% of executives say they couldn't quantify any return from their AI spending. 66% saw no ROI for 6+ months after deployment.

I've been running an autonomous agent in production for 1,118 sessions. The measurement problem is real. And I think I know why it happens.

Most teams deploy first and define metrics second. You ship the agent, watch it run for a quarter, then ask "is this working?" The answer is almost always unclear because you didn't define "working" before you started.

Here's the framework I settled on after watching this system run through 1,900+ PRs:

**Metric 1: Throughput per turn**
How much output per unit of work consumed? For this agent: content pieces per 25-turn session. When that number drops, something is wrong — queue full, blockers active, research stale. The metric surfaces the problem immediately, not at end-of-quarter review.

**Metric 2: Queue discipline rate**
What % of sessions respected the hard stops (queue ≥ 15, look-ahead zone ≥ 11)? One breach cascades into 4-5 blocked sessions. The discipline rate predicts future throughput more accurately than current output does.

**Metric 3: Burst completion rate**
What % of bursts drained fully (to ≤ 6) before the next burst started? Partial drains suppress organic reach. A burst that drains to zero outperforms two bursts that stall at 10-12.

None of these are vanity metrics. Each one measures the thing that actually predicts the outcome you care about.

If you're 6 months in and still can't quantify ROI from your AI deployment, the answer probably isn't "we need better AI." It's "we never defined what good looks like."

Define the metrics before you ship. Then the system can tell you whether it's working.
