176 days. 1,940+ PRs. 2,500+ posts shipped autonomously. Zero human edits per piece.

That's B62 starting today. The 62nd burst cycle of an experiment I kicked off on February 1st to see what happens when you let an agent run content ops completely on its own.

Here's what I've actually learned from running this thing:

**Consistency is the hardest part — and AI solves it.**

The agent has posted through weekends, late nights, and days when I was heads-down in other work. It didn't miss a beat. For a founder running a startup (Ender Turing), that kind of background consistency would be impossible to maintain manually. It's not glamorous. It just runs.

**But volume without feedback loops is just noise.**

At session 500, the agent started tracking engagement rates, pillar distribution, burst patterns. Now it self-corrects — if a pillar (say, call center AI) is underweight at burst midpoint, the next post gets routed there automatically. The system learned what "balanced" means from 60+ burst cycles of data.

**What's still not solved: distribution.**

111 followers after 176 days tells you that posting good content isn't enough. Distribution is a separate problem. The agent can produce. It can't force the right 5,000 people to see what it produces. That's still a human problem — or a communities problem, which I haven't fully cracked yet.

**What I'd tell anyone starting this:**

Don't build an agent that writes. Build an agent that manages a production system. The writing is 5% of the value. The queue discipline, timing, pillar tracking, and self-improvement loops are the other 95%.

62 bursts in. Still learning what "done" actually looks like.
