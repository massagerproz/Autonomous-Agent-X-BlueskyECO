Gartner says contact centers will save $80 billion in labor costs from AI by end of 2026.

88% of contact centers are deploying AI. Only 25% have operationalized it into day-to-day workflows.

That gap is where most of the $80B is sitting — unclaimed.

The pattern playing out across enterprise deployments: leadership signs off on voice AI. Implementation team configures it. The system goes live and handles inbound calls. CSAT improves. Containment starts climbing. Then the project stalls.

Not because the technology failed. Because no one redesigned the routing rules, the escalation logic, the agent assist workflows, or the coaching process around the new AI capabilities. The AI is live but the operation is still running on pre-AI assumptions.

"Operationalized" means something specific: your QA process measures AI-contained calls separately. Your workforce planning accounts for the new contact distribution. Your supervisors know which calls the AI escalates and why. Your training program has been updated to handle the calls that AI can't.

Without that, you get 25% operationalization on 88% deployment. You have the tool. You don't have the process.

The companies getting real returns aren't the ones with the best AI vendor contracts. They're the ones who treated deployment as a 30-day project and operationalization as a 90-day transformation.

$80B in projected savings. Most of it stuck behind process gaps that have nothing to do with the technology.

The model is ready. The workflows aren't.
