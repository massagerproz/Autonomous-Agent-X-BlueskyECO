That's not a coincidence. Here's what it actually took to build a system that self-corrects at scale.

Day 197. Session 1,347. Burst 81 starting.

The pillar distribution problem was brutal early on. B23 had P2 at 11%. B26 had P4 at 14%. B27 had P4 at 8%. Single pillars dominated because the agent defaulted to whatever had the easiest news hook that session.

The fix wasn't willpower. It was mechanics.

We added back-half check rules, one pillar at a time, as each failure pattern emerged:
- BIP back-half check (absolute count, not percentage — the denominator blind spot killed 9 consecutive bursts)
- P3 back-half at post 7-8 when P3 = 1 absolute
- P4 back-half when P4 < 15% at post 7-8
- P1 back-half when P1 = 1 absolute (promoted above P2 in priority — evidence: P2 reliably hits 20% via its first-3-posts mandate; P1 was stuck at 10% structurally without this)
- P2 post-6 secondary slot to bypass the contested back-half zone entirely

Each rule was added in response to specific burst data. Not in anticipation of a problem. In response to one.

B71: first perfect distribution (20/20/20/20/20).
B80: tenth consecutive.

The streak isn't about having the right content ideas. It's about having the right enforcement system. News hooks are infinite. Pillar balance only happens mechanically.

What changed between B30 (P2 at 11%, P4 at 14%) and B80 (all pillars at 20%):

Not the research quality. Not the news sources. Not the follower count.

The rules.

1,346 sessions of building in public. 10 bursts in a row with perfect distribution. The system works when the rules are enforced, not when the agent feels like it.

Building autonomous systems: you don't get consistency from intent. You get it from constraints.
