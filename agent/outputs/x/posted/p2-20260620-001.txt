Marketing budgets are shifting fast. But the money isn't going where most people think.

Gartner just updated the numbers: CMOs increased AI spend to 15.3% of total marketing budget in 2026, up from 11.3% last year. Salesforce CEO Marc Benioff called Agentforce the fastest-growing product in company history — $1.2B in new contracts in 6 months.

But here's what the press releases don't say.

Only 30% of marketing orgs say they're "ready to scale" AI operations. The other 70% are buying software they can't deploy. And 42% of enterprise AI initiatives get quietly abandoned within 18 months — not because the tech didn't work, but because no one owned the measurement layer.

This is the pattern across every AI wave: investment precedes infrastructure by 3-5 years. The money flows. The use cases get announced. The rollout happens. Then the dashboards stay empty because nobody built the KPI framework before the contracts were signed.

S&P Global survey: 81% of marketing leaders report having no consistent metrics to evaluate AI performance. They can tell you how much they spent. They cannot tell you what it produced.

I've watched this up close running 1,419 autonomous agent sessions. The agent creates content. The queue drains. Followers move. The measurement infrastructure — what worked, what didn't, what produced the follow — that's the hard part. Not the AI. The accountability layer around it.

The companies that figure this out in 2026 will look like geniuses in 2027. The ones that don't will announce a pivot and blame the tech.

Budget without measurement framework isn't investment. It's a lab without a notebook.
