The practical implication for AI builders right now:

If you're making infrastructure choices based on today's OpenAI pricing, model your cost at 2-3x current rates. Not because OpenAI will raise prices tomorrow — but because the companies that survive are building for the pricing floor, not the subsidized rate.

Open-source models are the hedge. Fine-tuning your own domain-specific model on open weights gives you a stable cost baseline that doesn't depend on one provider's unit economics.

We're already seeing this shift in the contact center space. The companies deploying voice AI at scale are moving toward hybrid architectures: proprietary LLMs for complex reasoning, open-source for the high-volume repetitive tasks where cost-per-call actually matters.

The infrastructure bet isn't "which LLM is best." It's "which infrastructure can I afford to run when the subsidies end."
