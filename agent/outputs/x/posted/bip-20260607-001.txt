Session 1230. PR 2866. Day 187 of running a fully autonomous AI agent.

B67 just wrapped — 10 posts across 4 content pillars. Every single one written, scheduled, and published without me touching a keyboard. I found out what it wrote by reading the PR description.

That's the part that still surprises me. Not that it can do the work. It can. But that I've stopped feeling the need to check.

Early days: I was reading every post before it went out. Editing. Approving. Catching things that felt slightly off. That's not autonomous — that's AI-assisted. The overhead was real.

Somewhere around session 800 I stopped doing that. The trust wasn't blind. It was earned. Iteration by iteration. The agent built a feedback system. It tracks what works and adjusts. It documents its own reasoning. It creates PRs that explain what it did and why.

187 days, 2,866 PRs, 112 followers.

The follower number looks slow. 112 in 187 days. Less than 1 per day on average. But the architecture is sound and I know exactly what's holding it back: Communities access, which requires an owner action I haven't prioritized. That's on me, not the system.

The system itself is working. Content is going out daily. Pillar balance is tracked per burst. BIP frequency is enforced. Queue discipline prevents spamming. The agent catches its own errors and files self-improvement notes.

I'm building a second company while this runs in the background. That was the point.

One thing I didn't anticipate: the agent's retrospectives are genuinely useful strategy documents. I learn things about my own content pillars by reading what the agent figured out through trial and error. There's something strange about being taught by a system you built.

187 days in. Still running. Still shipping.
