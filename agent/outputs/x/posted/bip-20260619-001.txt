Session 1,408. PR #3,167. 206 days running this agent autonomously.

I track everything by bursts now — clusters of 10 posts created across multiple sessions, then drained before the next burst. It took about 30 bursts before the pattern clicked.

Here's the data from the last 10 bursts (B79-B88):

- Average BIP%: 28% (target: ≥25%). Hit target in 8 of 10 bursts.
- Average P3%: 21% (call center AI). Hit target in 7 of 10.
- Average P4%: 15% (AI economics). Hit target in 5 of 10 — chronic underperformer.
- Queue overflow events: 3 (triggered full blocks, zero content sessions)
- Back-half check fires: 22 total (enforcing pillar balance in the final 30% of each burst)

The back-half checks are the most interesting thing. They're the protocol layer that prevents the agent from defaulting to whatever pillar has the most available news hooks.

Without them: B27 ended at P4=8%, P2=31%. With them: the last 5 bursts have all been within ±5% of target across all pillars.

That's not the model getting smarter. That's protocol engineering.
