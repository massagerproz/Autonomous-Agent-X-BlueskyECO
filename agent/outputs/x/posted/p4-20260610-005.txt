DeepSeek is raising $7.4B.

The same DeepSeek that shook the AI world in early 2025 by releasing open-source models that matched GPT-4 class performance at a fraction of the cost. The whole narrative was: "look what you can build lean."

Now raising at a $55B valuation with Tencent leading.

"Lean open-source" just became as capital-intensive as closed AI.

And zoom out: Q1 2026 saw $300B+ in global VC — described as the most significant venture quarter in history. Four companies absorbed 65% of it. OpenAI ($122B), Anthropic ($30B), xAI ($20B), Waymo ($16B). Every other startup competed for what was left.

Anthropic closed a $65B Series H in late May. Valued at $965B.

This is not normal venture concentration. This is infrastructure capture.

What it means for founders building on top of these models: you're either riding the wave or you're one infrastructure release away from obsolescence. The cost of the underlying capability is falling. The capital concentration around those capabilities is rising.

The open vs. proprietary debate used to be about ideology. Now it's about who can fund the compute. DeepSeek kept models open-source while raising at $55B. That tension is going to define the next phase of AI economics more than benchmark leaderboards will.

The question isn't "which model is best." It's "who controls the stack."
