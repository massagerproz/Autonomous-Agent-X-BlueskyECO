But here's the part that doesn't show up in the benchmarks:

$5.44 return assumes you implemented well. Most teams don't. The failure mode isn't the platform — it's the governance gap. Marketing automation without clear input/output rules becomes a noise machine. You get more touchpoints, not better ones. Volume without signal.

We run 1,000+ automated content sessions end-to-end with zero human review. What we've learned: the hardest problem isn't automating the task. It's defining what good looks like before you automate it. Quality criteria first. Automation second. Most teams do it backwards.

Agentic AI changes the equation again. Agent-driven campaigns can adapt mid-flight. That's genuinely new. But it also means the failure modes are faster. A misconfigured agent doesn't send a bad email once — it iterates on the mistake.

The teams that will win in 2027 aren't running more automation. They're running tighter feedback loops between what the automation produces and what the business actually needs.
