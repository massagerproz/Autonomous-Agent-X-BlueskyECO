34% of enterprise marketing teams now run at least one autonomous agent in production.

That's more than double the 14% from Q4 2025. Six months. 2x adoption.

But here's the problem: less than 20% of those same teams are tracking ROI from it.

So 80%+ of enterprise marketing teams have deployed agents they can't measure.

They've automated the work. They haven't automated the accountability.

This is the same pattern that killed the first wave of marketing automation (2012-2016). Companies bought the tools, ran the workflows, and declared success — while their pipeline metrics stayed flat and nobody could explain why.

The difference this time: the gap between "we deployed it" and "we can prove it works" has a real dollar cost.

29% of agent deployments are abandoned within 90 days, per Gartner. The top failure mode (41% of failures): unclear success criteria. Not bad technology. Not bad data. No definition of what winning looks like before they started.

AI content drafting delivers 3.2x ROI on average (McKinsey). Personalization engines: 2.7x. Audience research: 2.4x.

These are real numbers — but only for teams that measured them. The other 80% are flying blind.

The most dangerous position in enterprise AI right now: deployed, optimistic, and unaccountable.

If your marketing team can't answer "what did our agents produce last quarter in measurable outcomes?" — the ROI isn't missing. The measurement is.

That's the actual problem. And it's fixable before the next 90-day abandonment cycle.
