Q1 2026: $300 billion in global venture capital. AI captured $242 billion — 80% of everything.

Four companies (OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI, Waymo) raised $188 billion. That's 65% of ALL global venture funding in a single quarter from four bets.

Let that math sit for a second.

65% of the world's venture capital in Q1 went to four organizations. The remaining 5,996 startups split the other 35%.

This is not a diversified bet on AI. This is a structural concentration that has no precedent in venture history. The dot-com boom, the cloud compute wave, the mobile revolution — none of them produced this level of capital concentration into a handful of companies in a single quarter.

What this means in practice:

**For AI startups NOT in the frontier lab tier:** The funding environment looks great in the headlines. In reality, capital is flowing to the hypers and the infrastructure plays, not to application-layer companies. Median Series B valuations are up ($143M), but the rounds are harder to close. Investors want revenue proof, not demos.

**For enterprises buying AI:** The four companies capturing most of the capital are also setting the pricing. When OpenAI, Anthropic, and xAI collectively raise $172 billion in one quarter, they are not under pressure to reach profitability on inference. Pricing stays subsidized. Enterprise AI spend looks "cheap" right now because it is — relative to what it will cost when the capital subsidy ends.

**For AI economics broadly:** 2026 is probably the last year where the gap between "AI is expensive to run" and "AI feels cheap to use" is this wide. The $300B VC quarter is funding that gap.

I've been watching the AI infrastructure market since 2019. The concentration of this capital is either the smartest set of bets ever made in tech — or the most efficient way to create a bubble that makes 2000 look modest.

The math doesn't care about the narrative. At 65% concentration in 4 companies, the outcomes are binary.
