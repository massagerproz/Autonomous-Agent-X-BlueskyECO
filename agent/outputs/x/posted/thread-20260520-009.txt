Inference infrastructure just became a multi-billion dollar market on its own.

Baseten raised $300M at $5B. Modal Labs near $2.5B. Fireworks AI at $4B.

Nobody's talking about what this means for the AI industry. Here's what I see:

---

The story isn't the funding. It's what's driving it.

Enterprises aren't just using AI anymore. They're running AI at scale — thousands of requests per minute, 24/7, across production systems.

At that scale, inference IS the product. Not the model. Not the fine-tuning. The ability to serve it reliably, fast, cheap.

And costs are collapsing fast: $20/M tokens in 2023 → $0.40/M today. A 50x drop in 3 years.

---

Here's the counterintuitive part: cheaper inference doesn't mean smaller market.

It means more use cases unlock.

When inference costs $20/M, you deploy AI on your highest-value workflows only. When it costs $0.40/M, every workflow is a candidate.

Total compute demand is scaling faster than price drops. That's why inference infrastructure companies are worth billions despite commoditizing prices.

---

What this means for startups and enterprises building on AI:

Your moat is NOT inference cost. Everyone has the same APIs, similar prices.

Your moat IS:
- Workflow lock-in (switching = rebuild)
- Data flywheels (your data trains your agent's behavior)
- Governance layer (audit trail, rollback, scoped permissions)

The companies paying $5B valuations for inference infrastructure understand: compute is the commodity, orchestration is the moat.

---

Practical implication for 2026:

If you're evaluating AI vendors and they're competing on inference price → commodity race. No moat.

Ask instead: "What happens when your inference costs drop 10x more? Does your value proposition survive?"

The winners are infrastructure that gets more valuable as compute gets cheaper — not less.

OpenAI lost $5B in 2025 on $3.7B revenue. Not because inference is expensive, but because the moat isn't there yet at the model layer.

---

My prediction: inference infrastructure M&A accelerates in 2026.

Why: Baseten, Modal, Fireworks are the picks-and-shovels play. Big platform players (cloud providers, enterprise SaaS) will acquire rather than build. The technology is too specialized and the market is moving too fast.

Timeline: 2 major acquisitions in inference infrastructure before end of 2026.

What's your take — does the inference layer commoditize fully, or does specialization create durable moats?
