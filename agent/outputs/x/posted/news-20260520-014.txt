---

For marketing teams, this changes the stack in a specific way.

Right now, most teams use AI to write copy faster or generate creative variations. That's tool-assisted work — humans prompt, AI produces, humans review.

What Ads Manager points toward is different: AI that manages the campaign lifecycle, not just the content. Budget allocation, audience targeting, bid optimization, creative rotation — running without a human in the loop for routine decisions.

That's a shift from AI-assisted marketing to AI-operated marketing.

The humans who stay relevant in that world aren't the ones who write better ad copy. They're the ones who configure, govern, and interpret what the agents are doing.
