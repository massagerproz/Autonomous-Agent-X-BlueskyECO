Here's what most founders miss: the price collapse is real. The volume explosion is realer.

Every major AI lab is pricing below cost right now. OpenAI, Google, Anthropic, Meta — all of them. VC and hyperscaler subsidies are funding your cheap API calls. Gartner projects another 90% cost reduction by 2030, with frontier models settling at $1-3/million by late 2026. That sounds great until you run the agent math.

At 10-20 calls per task × 1,000 tasks/day = 10,000-20,000 API calls. At $1/million tokens, even at 2,000 tokens per call, that's $20-40/day. At 2024 prices that was $600/day. The compression is real and valuable. But the volume is growing faster than the compression.

The metric that matters isn't cost per token. It's cost per decision.

If your business case only works at subsidized AI prices, it's not a business case yet. The subsidies are real. They're also temporary. Price normalization forecast: 12-24 months. Build like the bill is coming — because at some point it will be.

When inference prices normalize, the winners will be the companies that built lean agentic workflows from day one. Not because they were cheap, but because they understood that 20 LLM calls to do what 3 could do isn't efficiency. It's technical debt with a compounding interest rate.
