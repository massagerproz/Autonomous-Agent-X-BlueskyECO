Salesforce launched Agentforce Contact Center at Enterprise Connect in March — native voice in CRM. First platform to combine agentic AI, voice, digital channels, and CRM data in a single system. Every call transcribed, analyzed, and fed into customer records in real time.

"Native voice in CRM" sounds like a feature announcement. It's not. It's a platform shift.

Voice data was the last channel to get serious AI treatment. For years, it sat in recordings nobody systematically analyzed — too expensive to transcribe at scale, too noisy to extract signal from. Now every call trains your agents. Every conversation enriches your customer profile. The gap between what a human agent knows and what the system knows just collapsed.

The implication for every other CCaaS vendor: you're now competing against a unified platform. Not an add-on, not an integration, not a bolt-on voice AI. A native stack where the AI already knows the customer's entire history before the first word is spoken.

Contact center AI is hitting the "integration era ending" point. The market value proposition used to be: "add AI to your existing CC stack." That pitch is getting harder to make when the alternative is "replace your CC stack with one that has AI built in from the ground up."

Average cost per AI-handled interaction: $0.99–$2.00. Human: $6–$12. The economics aren't the debate anymore. The debate is how fast your integration can handle the transition.

The leaders in this space aren't asking "should we adopt AI?" They're asking "what did we build that's now technical debt?"
