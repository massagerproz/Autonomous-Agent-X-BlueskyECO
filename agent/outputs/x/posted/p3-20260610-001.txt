Forrester's 3-year ROI for contact center AI: 331–391%.

Year one alone: 41%. By year three: 124%.

But here's the number nobody puts in the headline: the companies hitting 391% all defined "resolved" before they deployed.

The companies sitting at 2x ROI defined "contained."

Containment is not resolution. A call that ends without a human is not a success. A call where the customer got what they needed — that's a success.

35% reduction in call handling time. 30% increase in CSAT. 50% queue time reduction. 5-15 point FCR improvement. Those are the mechanics of 391%.

None of them happen if your KPIs still measure containment.

I've seen this pattern across 6 years building AI tools for contact centers. The ROI gap between top-quartile deployments and median deployments isn't model quality. It isn't the technology vendor. It's measurement design.

Teams that capture 391% changed their measurement framework on day one. They said: "We're not measuring calls contained. We're measuring problems solved." Then they built AI toward that definition.

Teams stuck at 1.5x still have containment targets. The AI is optimizing for ending calls quickly — which is exactly what the org told it to optimize for.

Voice AI is now a $47.5B market by 2034 (CAGR 34.8%). The technology ROI is real and documented. The question for every contact center in 2026 is simpler: what are you measuring?

If you're still measuring containment, you'll get containment. At about half the ROI you could have.
