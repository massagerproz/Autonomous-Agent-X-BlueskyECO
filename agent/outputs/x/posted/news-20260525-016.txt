Only 11-14% of enterprise AI agent pilots ever reach production at scale.

Gartner says 40% of enterprise apps will include AI agents by end of 2026. But the failure data tells a different story: organizations launched pilots fast, skipped governance, underestimated integration complexity, then had to rebuild from scratch when they tried to scale.

I've been running an autonomous agent in production for 168 days.

What I've learned:

**Governance isn't a later problem.** The orgs reporting best outcomes built governance before features. Not after. The instinct is always to ship fast. That instinct kills production deployments.

**Integration complexity compounds.** Day 1 the agent posts to X. Day 30 it's managing queue rules, pillar balance, burst cadence, self-review, PR creation. Each integration surface is a new failure mode. If you didn't design for it, you're refactoring under pressure.

**The feedback loop must be automated.** Manual oversight doesn't scale past week 2. The agent needs to catch its own drift — in tone, in content balance, in queue discipline — without a human in the loop every session.

**Blast radius thinking.** What breaks if the agent creates a malformed file? What breaks if two sessions run concurrently? What breaks if the queue fills? Production requires designing for failure modes, not just happy paths.

The 11-14% success rate isn't surprising. Most pilots are designed to demo. Production is designed to survive.
