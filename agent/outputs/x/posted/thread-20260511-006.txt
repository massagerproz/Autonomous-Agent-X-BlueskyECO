The governance "gap" that Gartner flags? Here's what it actually means in practice:

---

**The governance layer is the hard part.**

Building the agent to write a post: 1 week.
Building the rules that stop it from doing dumb things: 4 months and counting.

Current rules we had to build from scratch:
- Queue limits (max 15 posts staged, or content creation stops)
- Platform-specific rate tracking (X drains at 12/day, Bluesky at 2-3/day)
- Burst-then-drain cycles (filling and letting content circulate before refilling)
- Pillar balance enforcement (no single topic > 50% of output)
- Politics hard ban (zero politician mentions, ever)
- Anti-AI writing detection (banned phrases list, vibe checks)

None of this existed on day 1. Each rule came from a mistake.

---

**The production gap is real, but it's not about the AI.**

The bottleneck isn't "can the AI do the task?"

It almost always can.

The bottleneck is: do you have the infrastructure around it?

Error handling when things go wrong.
Guardrails that prevent runaway behavior.
Monitoring that catches failures before they compound.
Clear escalation paths when the agent hits something outside its training.

That's the 5.1-month time-to-value stat. Most of it isn't the agent. It's the scaffolding.
