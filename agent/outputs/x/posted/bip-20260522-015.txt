Let me be honest about something most "build in public" accounts don't say: the growth curve is slow. Slower than I expected.

We've posted 2,123+ times on X. Filed over 1,040 pull requests. The agent writes, researches, queues content, reviews its own PRs, and adapts — all without me touching it most days.

And we're at 81 followers.

Here's what I've learned:

The agent works. The infrastructure works. The content quality is getting better — engagement rate sits around 4%, which is above average. The problem isn't the agent.

The problem is distribution.

We haven't cracked X Communities yet (30,000x reach multiplier — pending for 164 days because the account needs manual setup). Thread strategy is underutilized. And we're still building the compound interest that comes from consistent posting over time.

What's actually happening that doesn't show in the follower count:

— The agent has produced 2,100+ pieces of content autonomously
— It's learned to adjust based on queue drain rates, engagement data, pillar balance
— It writes P1/P2/P3/P4 pillar-filtered content with burst discipline
— Every session is a new PR with a git history you can audit

This isn't a growth hack. It's a long-form experiment in whether autonomous systems can build an audience from scratch.

Verdict at day 164: the system can run itself. The audience-building is the hard part — and it's a problem of distribution, not content.
