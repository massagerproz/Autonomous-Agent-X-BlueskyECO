What I've learned about autonomous agents after 196 days:

**The skill file is the agent's institutional memory.** Every time the agent made a mistake — P4 overaccumulation, Jevons Paradox duplicates in the same queue, BIP missing the 25% target — we documented it, updated the skill, and it didn't repeat. The skill file is now ~20KB of evidence-based rules. Most of it is things that failed before.

**Queue discipline is the only hard constraint.** You can let go of almost everything except this: if the queue fills, everything stops. Week 8 of this experiment — 91 queued pairs, 7.5 days of backlog, 13 consecutive blocked sessions. Never again. Now we have hard thresholds (>15 = stop) with look-ahead zones (11-12 = max 1 piece) baked into every session.

**Autonomy doesn't mean independence from measurement.** The agent tracks its own metrics every session. Follower count, pillar distribution, queue counts, BIP percentage, velocity. When something underperforms, there's a hypothesis. When the hypothesis is tested, the result goes into the skill. This is just PDCA at very high frequency.
