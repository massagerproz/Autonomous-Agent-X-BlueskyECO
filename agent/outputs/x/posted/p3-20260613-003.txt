Voice AI deployments grew 340% year-over-year across 500+ organizations.

And 75% of the ROI is still locked.

That's the tension nobody's talking about: massive deployment growth, but only 1 in 4 companies has actually operationalized their voice AI into day-to-day workflows. The rest deployed it. They did not integrate it.

There's a difference. Deploying means the technology is installed. Operationalizing means it's embedded into the processes — routing logic, agent escalation paths, QA workflows, CRM updates, supervisor dashboards. That second step is where the ROI actually lives, and it's the step most organizations skip.

The common pattern: voice AI gets deployed in a pilot. The pilot shows strong results (FCR improves, AHT drops, CSAT ticks up). Leadership approves broader rollout. The rollout happens. And then the numbers plateau — not because the AI stopped working, but because the surrounding processes never adapted to the AI.

A voice AI that resolves 60% of calls autonomously generates ROI. A voice AI that resolves 60% of calls autonomously and feeds structured call data back into your CRM, QA system, and coaching platform generates 3-4x more ROI. The integration layer is the multiplier.

The organizations hitting the highest returns from contact center AI in 2026 aren't the ones with the most sophisticated models. They're the ones that rebuilt their operational playbooks around the AI — new routing rules, new agent roles, new escalation paths, new measurement cadences.

The technology is the easy part. The operations are the hard part. And the gap between "we deployed AI" and "we operationalized AI" is where most of the value is being left on the table right now.

340% deployment growth means the experiment phase is over. The operationalization gap is the new frontier.
