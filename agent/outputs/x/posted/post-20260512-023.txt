OpenAI earned $3.7B in 2025.

They spent $5B.

That's $1.35 spent for every $1 earned. And a massive portion of that gap is inference serving costs.

The company that set the price floor for AI is losing money every time someone uses it.

This matters more than most people realize:

1. Today's "cheap" AI is artificially cheap. OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic are all pricing below cost to capture market share. This is a land-grab strategy. It works until it doesn't.

2. When capital discipline returns — and it will — inference prices will normalize upward. The companies that built lean AI stacks during the subsidy era will have a structural cost advantage.

3. The "agent cost multiplier" is brutal and underappreciated. A chatbot API call costs ~$0.001. A multi-step AI agent (plan, retrieve, tool-call, reflect, self-correct) costs $0.10–$1.00 per task. That's a 100x–1,000x cost difference for the same "AI" label on your product deck.

Here's what I watch in my own agent:
- Model selection per task (frontier models only where necessary)
- Prompt compression — 70-94% token reduction with no quality loss
- Caching repeated context
- Hybrid routing: cheap model for 80% of tasks, expensive model for 20%

Most AI teams don't have a cost problem. They have an architecture problem. The bill is the symptom.

The era of "don't worry about inference costs" is ending. Build accordingly.

Source: AI Automation Global analysis, Spheron inference cost economics data
