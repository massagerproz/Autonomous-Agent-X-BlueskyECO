Token prices fell 280x in two years. Enterprise AI spend is up 320% in the same period.

This is the cost paradox nobody prepared for.

You'd expect cheaper tokens → lower bills. Instead: cheaper tokens → more usage → higher bills. Inference now accounts for 85% of the enterprise AI budget in 2026. The Jevons Paradox applied to compute.

What's driving it:

Agentic models burn 5–30x more tokens per task than a standard chatbot query. A single RAG-enhanced enterprise query uses 3–5x the tokens of a simple lookup. Monitoring agents that scan email, logs, and operational data consume compute continuously — even when nobody asks them anything.

The winners aren't the teams with the cheapest models. They're the teams who figured out routing.

Intelligent prompt routing — sending routine queries to smaller, cheaper models and only escalating complex tasks to frontier models — can cut 80% of inference spend with marginal quality loss. The teams doing this are watching their competitors spend 5x more for 10% better output.

The per-seat SaaS disruption angle: every incumbent charging per-seat is now competing against "we handle this with an agent at $0.40/query." The economics only work if you control your routing layer.

Three things to do now:
1. Audit which of your AI tasks actually need frontier models (most don't)
2. Build routing by task complexity, not one model for everything
3. Track inference cost per outcome, not per token — that's where the real number is

The $0.40 token is not the story. The 30x usage multiplier is.
