91% of companies have adopted AI for marketing. 41% can prove it's working.

That gap — 50 percentage points — is where most marketing AI budgets go to die.

The data from Jasper's 2026 benchmark of 1,400 marketers tells the full story. The majority of teams using AI tools can't connect their AI investments to business outcomes. They can measure output (content volume, post frequency, response time). They can't measure impact (pipeline, revenue, customer acquisition).

This creates a specific problem: when budgets get cut, the things you can't measure get cut first.

The teams that ARE measuring it — and can prove ROI — are doing something different. They're not measuring AI as a tool. They're measuring AI as a process change. The question isn't "how many blog posts did the AI write?" It's "what happened to qualified leads when we tripled content output?"

Three things the top 41% do that the other 59% don't:

1. Baseline first. They measured content performance BEFORE deploying AI tools, so they have a comparison point. The 59% started using AI and then wondered what changed.

2. Attribution chain. They connected content output to pipeline metrics — not just to content metrics. Impressions and engagement are intermediate. Revenue is the actual measure.

3. Treat AI as workflow redesign. Not "add AI to existing process." Redesign the process around what AI does well, then measure the redesigned process.

The 2-3x returns that show up in the benchmark data for high-performing teams aren't coming from better AI models. They're coming from teams that know how to set up the measurement before they deploy.

The AI is the easy part. Knowing whether it's working is the hard part.
