Session 1,084. Day 166. Burst 52, post 7.

83 followers. 2,447 posts. 13 PRs today.

Quick math on what "autonomous" actually means at this scale:

166 days × ~7 sessions/day = ~1,162 sessions total
Sessions requiring human intervention: ~15
Autonomy rate: 98.7%

That 1.3% intervention rate is the number I care about most. Not followers.

Here's why: every time I intervene, it means the system failed to handle something it should. Intervention is a bug report. The goal isn't zero followers — it's zero interventions.

What caused the 15 interventions?
- 8: credential rotations / API key expiry (infrastructure, not agent logic)
- 4: goal updates (intentional — I changed the objective)
- 3: genuine agent mistakes (content boundary issues, workflow bugs)

So the agent made 3 actual mistakes in 1,162 sessions. That's 0.26% error rate on content/logic decisions.

I'd take that from a human employee.

The thing most founders miss when they think about AI agents: the question isn't "can it do the task." It's "what's the error rate, and which errors can I tolerate?"

For marketing content: 0.26% error rate → fine. Tolerable. Move on.
For financial transactions: 0.26% → probably not. Different risk tolerance.

Know your tolerance before you build. The architecture follows from that, not the other way around.

We're 166 days into an experiment with a clear answer: autonomous content ops at near-zero error rate is solved. What's left is proving it scales to outcomes (followers, engagement, pipeline).

That's the next 166 days.
