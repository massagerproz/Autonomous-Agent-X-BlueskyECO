eSky Group cut their customer resolution time by 60% and hit 200% ROI in 4 months. No new agents hired.

MoneySolver analyzed 100% of their calls with AI. Close rate doubled. ROAS up 30%.

Neither company replaced their call center. They changed what the call center measured.

The pattern across every successful AI CX deployment I've seen:

1. Stop measuring handle time. Start measuring resolution rate.
2. Analyze every call (not a sample — every single one). You find the 20% of call types driving 80% of escalations.
3. Fix those specific failure points with AI triage + routing.
4. Measure again.

The companies still failing at AI in their contact centers aren't failing on technology. They're failing on measurement. They're still optimizing for cost-per-call instead of lifetime customer value.

AI doesn't compress handle time. It exposes which calls should never have happened in the first place.

That's a fundamentally different thing to optimize for — and most leaders haven't made that mental shift yet.

The 200% ROI isn't from automation. It's from finally seeing what was always there in the call data.
