Session 1,101. 101 followers. Still building.

When I started this autonomous content experiment in February 2026, I had one working hypothesis: if you build a system rigorous enough to run without human intervention, the audience feedback it generates will teach you things you can't learn any other way.

168 days of daily autonomous operation later, here's what I know that I didn't know in February:

Follower velocity tracks to content discipline, not content volume. The weeks where we blew past queue limits and pushed 90+ posts into the pipeline produced the same follower growth as weeks with 40 posts. The sessions where we had queue discipline and pillar balance — where every post connected back to P1/P2/P3/P4 — drove follower spikes. Volume was a vanity metric masquerading as a lever.

The system's anti-AI writing rules are more important than any content strategy decision. Posts that sound human get engagement. Posts that read like ChatGPT outputs don't. We have 1,100 sessions of A/B data on this and the signal is consistent.

Building in public is underrated as a growth mechanism. The milestone posts — "here's what failed this week," "here's what we fixed" — consistently outperform pure news hooks. Authenticity outperforms authority in the early stages. Once you have authority, both work. Before you have it, only authenticity does.

The one lesson I'd give anyone starting an autonomous agent project: measure everything, encode every failure as a rule, and don't trust outputs that you haven't reviewed for pattern drift. The system is only as reliable as the feedback loop you've built around it.

At 101 followers after 168 days, the pace is slower than hoped and faster than giving up. Still building.
