OpenAI is projected to lose $14 billion in 2026.

They spend $1.35 for every dollar they earn. Some analysts put it higher — $3.30 to make $1.00. The mechanism: inference costs. Every ChatGPT response, every API call, every GPT-5.4 extended-context window costs more to serve than they charge for it. Deliberately priced below true cost to capture market share.

This is the unit economics problem nobody building on top of frontier AI wants to talk about.

Here's why it matters for your AI strategy:

Those $0.006/million token prices you're budgeting around? They exist because the model labs are subsidizing your usage. OpenAI, Anthropic, Google — all running inference at a loss to prevent each other from winning the developer ecosystem. That subsidy doesn't last forever.

A Turing Award-winning Google researcher published a paper in early 2026 identifying inference costs as the primary economic bottleneck preventing AI companies from reaching profitability. The finding: context window length scales quadratically with compute cost. Every token in a 1M-token context gets attended to on every generation step. The cost of frontier reasoning isn't falling — it's rising per query.

The Gartner March 2026 analysis confirms it: agentic AI requires 5-30x more tokens per task than standard chatbots. You're not buying chatbot economics. You're buying agent economics. The math is different.

What changes when subsidies end (12-24 month runway per most analysts):
- Frontier model API prices rise for high-context, high-reasoning tasks
- Enterprises that built on flat-rate predictions will face budget surprises
- The "route 80% of traffic to cheaper models" playbook becomes mandatory, not optional

The enterprises building for this now — model routing, semantic caching, task decomposition to reduce per-task token load — are going to look smart. The ones budgeting 2027 AI projects on 2026 subsidized pricing are not.

Token prices have fallen 10x in 18 months. Total enterprise AI spend has risen 320% in the same window. Both facts are true simultaneously. That's not a contradiction — it's the structure of an industry running on investor-subsidized economics.

Plan accordingly.
