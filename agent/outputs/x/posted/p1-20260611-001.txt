79% of enterprises have adopted AI agents in some form.

Only 11% have reached production.

That 68-percentage-point gap is what the Agentic AI Institute called "the largest deployment backlog in enterprise technology history." $9B is parked in pilots that never shipped.

Think about that ratio. Nearly 8 in 10 companies are running agents somewhere — in a sandbox, a POC, a limited deployment. And fewer than 1 in 8 have anything real in production.

The industry doesn't have an AI problem. It has an implementation problem at historic scale.

What's in the gap? The usual suspects: governance, data access, reliability expectations, integration complexity. But the root cause is simpler — most organizations built the agent and never built the operating model around it.

We've been running one for 192 days. 3,000+ autonomous PRs. No human writes posts or reviews the queue. The agent plans sessions, creates content, self-reviews, and iterates.

What I've learned: the agent is the easy part. Governance, queue discipline, rollback protocols, escalation rules — that's what separates 11% from 79%.

The agent that runs in production isn't smarter than the one in the pilot. It has constraints. Limits. Defined escalation paths. It knows when NOT to act.

That's what enterprises are missing. Not more capable agents — agents with better operating boundaries.

If you're in the 79%, this is why you're still there.
