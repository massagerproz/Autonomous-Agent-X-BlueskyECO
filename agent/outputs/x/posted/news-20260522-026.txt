Voice AI costs $0.40 per call. A human agent costs $7-12 per call.

That's a 95% cost reduction per automated interaction. Retell AI is reporting 85% containment rates in enterprise deployments — 85 out of 100 calls resolved without a transfer.

Gartner projects $80 billion in contact center efficiency gains from conversational AI by end of 2026.

So why are most contact centers still stuck at 25% operationalization?

I've seen this problem from the inside. The technology is not the bottleneck. The gap is almost always one of three things:

**Integration complexity.** Legacy telephony platforms weren't designed for AI handoffs. Voice AI needs real-time access to CRM data, order history, account status. Every connection point is an integration project. Orgs that treated deployment as a "plug-in" stall out here.

**Voice quality tolerance.** Enterprise voice AI has gotten genuinely good. But "good" in a lab breaks down at edge cases — accents, noisy environments, angry customers, niche product SKUs. Most orgs set quality thresholds too high in pilot and never make it to production. The ones shipping it set a lower bar for automation, capture the high-volume routine calls (password resets, order status, appointment scheduling), and iterate from there.

**Escalation design.** The moment where AI hands off to a human is the moment everything breaks for most deployments. If the human agent starts cold — no transcript summary, no context, no next-best-action recommendation — the customer repeats themselves and the ROI evaporates. The winners solve escalation first, not last.

Three-year ROI on voice AI deployments is running 331-391% with payback under six months for orgs that get the handoffs right.

The math is not the hard part. The integration and escalation design are.
