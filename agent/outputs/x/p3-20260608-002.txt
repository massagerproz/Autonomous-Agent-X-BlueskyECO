88% of contact centers have deployed AI. Only 25% have operationalized it.

That gap — 63 percentage points — represents trillions of dollars in locked ROI.

The hard part was never the technology. Everyone figured that out. The hard part is what comes after deployment: workflow redesign, agent coaching, QA governance, escalation logic, feedback loops. All the boring operational work that doesn't show up in vendor demos.

Here's what I see at Ender Turing, working with contact centers daily:

Most deployments stall at the same three points:

1. **No closed loop between AI and human performance** — AI flags issues, but nobody acts on them systematically. QA scores improve on paper. CSAT doesn't move.

2. **Agents aren't trained to work with AI** — They work around it. Or ignore the prompts. The tech is live but adoption is 20-30%. You're paying for full capacity and using a quarter of it.

3. **Leadership KPIs didn't change** — Management still measures handle time and tickets closed. AI that reduces those numbers looks bad on the old dashboard. So nobody champions it internally.

The economics are brutal when you look at what's on the table: human agents cost $7-12 per call. Voice AI costs $0.40. That's a 95% cost reduction per automated interaction. One Forrester study documented $10.3M in labor savings over 3 years, 331-391% ROI, payback under 6 months.

That ROI is sitting there. Uncaptured. In 63% of organizations that already have the technology running.

This isn't a technology problem. It's a governance and change management problem.

The contact centers that crack operationalization in the next 12 months will have an insurmountable competitive advantage over the ones still doing demos.
