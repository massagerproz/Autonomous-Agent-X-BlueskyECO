Q1 2026: $242 billion in AI funding. 80% of all global venture capital.

5% of enterprises see real ROI from AI.

That ratio should bother more people.

---

Here's what's actually happening. The capital is rational. The early returns from infrastructure players — OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI — are real. Foundation models are compounding. The $122B OpenAI round isn't speculation; it's a bet that the infrastructure layer captures everything above it.

But enterprise adoption has a completely different story. Only 8% of companies report measurable returns. 95% are in the "we're investing, figuring it out" category. The three barriers killing ROI aren't what you'd expect:

1. Data access — 50% of enterprises say they can't get AI where their data actually lives
2. Privacy/compliance — 44% are blocked by governance requirements they haven't solved yet
3. Data quality — 40% say the data isn't clean enough to trust the outputs

This isn't an AI problem. It's a data infrastructure problem that the AI investment wave exposed.

---

The business model implication is real: $285 billion in SaaS market cap has already been repriced since January. Not because AI delivered on every promise — because enterprises started reducing software seats rather than adding them. The disruption worked before the replacements were fully ready.

That's a dangerous gap. Companies cutting Salesforce seats and Teams licenses to fund AI tools, before the AI tools can do what the old tools did.

---

My read on where this goes:

The 5% that achieve ROI all share a pattern: they solved governance and data access BEFORE deploying AI at scale. They didn't buy the shiny tools first. They built the data foundation.

The next 18 months will separate companies by whether they address the 50/44/40 problem (data access/compliance/quality) or keep chasing capability announcements.

$242B in funding says the AI infrastructure is being built. The enterprise ROI data says the adoption infrastructure isn't ready yet.

That gap is where the next wave of real value creation happens. Not in foundation models — in the connective tissue that gets enterprise data ready for AI to run on.
