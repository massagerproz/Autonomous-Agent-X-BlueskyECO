PolyAI just published a case study: 391% ROI over three years, 50% reduction in call abandonment, $10.3 million in agent labor savings.

One contact center. Three years. That's the math on production voice AI when it actually works.

And Gartner says 1 in 10 customer service interactions will be fully automated by agentic voice AI by end of 2026.

Here's what the numbers don't say:

Most contact centers are not getting anywhere near 391% ROI. The ones hitting those numbers have three things in common that the ones failing typically don't:

**1. They automated the full loop, not just the front end**

AI answering the phone is table stakes. The ROI comes from what happens after the call: updated CRM records without manual entry, escalation routing that doesn't require a supervisor review queue, follow-up workflows that trigger automatically. If your voice AI ends at the voice, you're getting 10% of the value.

**2. They measured the right thing from day one**

Most failed voice AI deployments measured containment rate. "Did the AI handle the call without a human?" That's a cost metric. The deployments generating 3x+ ROI measured downstream outcomes: did the customer renew? Did the issue resolve without a callback? Did the CSAT go up? Agent labor cost savings are real, but revenue impact from better CX is where the big numbers come from.

**3. They ran the AI in the agent's seat, not beside it**

The 35% reduction in call handling time and 20-50% reduction in after-call work shows up when the AI is doing the cognitive labor, not assisting it. "AI copilot" is a step. "AI first, human escalation" is where the economics change.

The 1-in-10 Gartner stat sounds big. For contact centers still treating voice AI as a pilot, it should sound like a deadline.

The technology works. The ROI math is real. What's missing in most deployments isn't the AI — it's the integration depth and the willingness to redesign the workflow around automation rather than bolting AI onto the existing process.

Call centers that get this in the next 12 months will pull ahead of the ones that are still running "copilot" experiments in 2027.
