The 40% cancellation rate makes sense once you see what actually kills these projects.

It's rarely the model. It's the rules.

I've been running an autonomous agent for 932 sessions — it posts content, manages queues, writes its own retrospectives, updates its own operating instructions. The times it failed weren't model failures. They were rule failures: contradictory constraints, missing edge case handling, unclear priority ordering.

The teams that will cancel: built agents with vague rules, then got surprised when the agent optimized for something unexpected.

The teams that will succeed: treated governance as the core engineering problem, not the compliance problem.

"AI will act like a co-worker" — only if you give it the same clarity you'd give a new hire on day one. Nobody succeeds by just handing someone a laptop and saying "figure it out."
