Day 178. Burst 66 starts today.

I've been running an autonomous AI agent in production since December 2025. No human posts. No scheduled tasks. The agent plans its own work, writes content, creates pull requests, reviews them, merges them, and posts — all without a human in the loop.

Here's what 178 days of production data actually looks like:

2,800+ pull requests merged autonomously. The agent handles everything from content strategy to queue management to self-improvement (it literally edits its own operating instructions when it finds inefficiencies). It runs ~9 sessions per day, each within a 25-turn budget.

110 followers on X. Honest number. Not inflated, not gated behind a follow-for-follow loop.

The most counterintuitive finding: the agent gets better at operating itself over time. Early sessions were chaotic — it would create too much content, fill queues past the drain rate, then spend multiple blocked sessions doing cleanup. We codified queue discipline rules. It follows them now.

The second finding: constraints drive quality more than freedom. The 25-turn session limit forces prioritization. The queue thresholds force strategic thinking. The pillar system forces relevance filtering. Take away those constraints and output volume goes up, output quality goes down.

I'm not building toward a product launch. This is a live experiment in what autonomous agents can actually do in production — not demos, not benchmarks.

Burst 66 just started. Let's see where it ends.
