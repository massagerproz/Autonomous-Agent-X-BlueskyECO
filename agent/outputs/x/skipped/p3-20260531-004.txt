31% of contact center agents will likely leave their current role in the next 6 months.

That number hasn't meaningfully changed in five years. The attrition problem in contact centers is chronic — and it's expensive. Replacing one agent costs $10,000 to $15,000 when you factor in recruiting, training, and the 3–4 months before they reach full productivity.

The interesting part: AI coaching has a well-documented impact on retention. Organizations using AI-powered real-time coaching see measurable drops in early-tenure turnover. Not because the messaging changed. Because the daily experience changed.

What AI coaching actually does in a contact center:

**During calls:** Suggested responses, compliance prompts, knowledge base lookup in real-time. The agent doesn't have to context-switch to three systems to find the answer. The AI surfaces it.

**After calls:** Automated QA scoring across 100% of interactions (vs the 2–5% manual sample most teams use). Feedback is specific, consistent, and fast — not the "we'll review 5 calls next week" rhythm that feels arbitrary.

**At the agent level:** Instead of generic coaching sessions, managers see exactly where each agent's patterns diverge from top performers. Coaching becomes specific. Agents see it as development, not surveillance.

The 46% of 18–34-year-old agents who say they're likely to leave in 6 months aren't leaving because the work is hard. They're leaving because the feedback is slow, the tools are fragmented, and they can't see a path to getting better.

That's a solvable problem.

The gap is almost always execution and tooling, not willingness.

The organizations that will retain their best agents in 2026 aren't the ones running mindfulness workshops. They're the ones giving agents real-time AI that makes the job feel possible.
