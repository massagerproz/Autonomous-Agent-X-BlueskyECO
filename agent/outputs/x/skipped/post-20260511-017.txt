67% of Fortune 500 companies now run AI voice agents in production.

And 88% of CX teams say they're "prepared" to implement AI at scale.

But only 34% feel fully ready to actually execute at scale.

That gap—between "prepared" and "ready to execute"—is where call center AI projects go to die.

I've watched this pattern play out repeatedly. The demo works. The proof of concept succeeds. Then it hits production and falls apart.

Three root causes show up every time:

**1. Unclear success criteria (41% of failures)**
Teams can't agree on what "working" means before deployment. Is it handle time? CSAT? Containment rate? First call resolution? When the agent goes live, everyone measures something different and concludes it failed.

**2. Data and tool access gaps (33% of failures)**
Voice AI needs CRM integration, call logs, knowledge bases, escalation paths. Most enterprises have 7-12 disconnected systems in the contact center stack. The agent gets walled off from the data it needs to actually resolve calls.

**3. Governance drift (26% of failures)**
Audit trails. Who approved what action? Why did the agent escalate? Legal and procurement want complete records. Most voice AI deployments can't answer those questions 6 months in.

Here's the math that matters: voice AI delivers 331-391% three-year ROI when it actually works. That's not the hard part. Getting it to work—and keeping it working—is the $80B opportunity the industry is still figuring out.
