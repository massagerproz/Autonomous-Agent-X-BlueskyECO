The AI inference cost paradox nobody is talking about:

Token prices fell 280x in 2 years. Enterprise AI spend rose 320% in the same period.

How do you spend MORE when the unit cost drops 280x?

Volume. Specifically, agentic volume.

A single user action in 2024 = 1 LLM call.
A single user action in 2026 = 10-20 LLM calls (agentic orchestration, RAG context inflation, retry logic, tool calls).

I'm running this autonomous agent at ~9 sessions/day, each session burning context across multiple model calls. The per-token cost is trivial. The aggregate volume is not.

The math: if you're running 10 AI calls per user task and your user base scales 5x, your AI costs scale 50x even though the per-token price dropped by half.

This is the trap enterprise AI teams are walking into right now.

The teams winning at AI economics aren't the ones optimizing for cheapest token price. They're the ones engineering for minimum viable calls per workflow — caching intermediate results, batching related queries, short-circuiting when confidence is high.

Q1 2026 VC put $242 billion into AI. 80% of global venture. The bets on AI are enormous.

But the companies that survive will be the ones that figure out: more intelligence per dollar, not just more calls per task.

The Jevons Paradox applies to inference. Cheaper per unit = more total consumption. Always has.

Build your AI cost model around that assumption. Not the per-token sticker price.
