Token prices dropped 280x in two years. Enterprise AI bills went up 320%.

That's not a paradox. That's Jevons paradox. When something gets cheaper, usage expands until the total cost goes up. Happened with electricity. Happened with cloud compute. Now it's happening with LLM tokens.

Here's the math:
- LLM token costs fell ~80% from 2025 to 2026 alone
- Three-year drop: roughly 280x
- Average enterprise AI budget: $1.2M/year in 2024 → $7M in 2026
- AI inference costs now represent 85% of enterprise AI budgets

Open-source challengers like DeepSeek V3.2 run at $0.14 per million tokens input. Claude Opus 4 runs at $15 per million. That's a 100x+ price spread within a single model generation.

And OpenAI, the company that set this off, lost $1.35 for every dollar it earned. $3.7B revenue, ~$5B loss. Sora burned $15M/day in inference costs against $2.1M in lifetime revenue before it was shut down.

The labs are pricing below cost to capture market share. The assumption is they'll figure out the unit economics later.

For enterprises building on top of this: you're not buying stability. You're buying market share battles subsidized by VC money. When the subsidies end, the prices normalize. Plan accordingly.

The smarter play: build workflows where you control token efficiency from day one. The cost per useful output — not cost per token — is the metric that matters. Any autonomous system that can measure that has a real advantage when pricing normalizes.

Because it will normalize.
